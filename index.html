The Approach

01
Understand before building

Ask why until you understand what's actually broken. The problem is 80% of the work. Rushing this is where most projects die.

02
PRD before a single line of code

Prototype your way to clarity. A clear understanding of the solution is the brief. Speed comes next.

03
Iterate fast, measure everything

Ship the smallest working thing. The ability to iterate is the real advantage — not the first build.

04
Minimum effective dose

Most organizations don't need more AI tools. They need fewer, aimed more precisely at the right problem.

AI Tutor · Personal Build

The Upside Down Study Hall

A bright kid with ADHD gives the first answer, not the best answer. Every Sunday, the gap between “done” and “prepared” compounds. Generic tutoring tools don’t know his curriculum or care about depth.

The backstory Built for my son’s Grade 6 Sunday prep. Upload the week’s syllabus (photo, PDF, paste), and Claude extracts topics mapped to real Common Core/NGSS standards. Each mission has 3 progressively harder questions in Stranger Things voice, followed by a mandatory open-ended follow-up — the depth mechanic. Dustin doesn’t let you off with “idk.”

Standards-first curriculum model: Claude’s training knowledge is the backbone, school materials are the overlay. Every topic maps to a named standard code with source links on the parent summary. Anti-confusion guardrails ensure wrong answers are clearly wrong — confidence-building, not trick questions. File extraction uses Claude’s document processing (not third-party parsers) for reliable PDF/image handling on serverless. PRD to deployed product in one session.

Personal Automation · Family

FamilyOps — Always-On Family Chief of Staff

Single dad, two boys, co-parenting logistics. School emails, calendar conflicts, weekly family plans — it all needs coordinating and nobody's doing it.

Processes school emails, updates Google Calendar automatically, generates printable weekly family plans for the fridge. Runs autonomously with duplicate protection and two-input handling for simple vs. complex schedules.

The backstory Version 1 was a Python script run from the terminal. Worked well for months. Then I moved it to OpenClaw to make it always-on — no terminal, no manual trigger. Now it's a family chief of staff that runs in the background. The problem was personal. The solution taught me about building autonomous agents.
